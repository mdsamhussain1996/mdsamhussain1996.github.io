Research Profile

Academic Focus

My research lies at the intersection of nonlinear dynamical systems, fractional calculus, and neural network modeling. I focus on controllability, synchronization, and stability of fractional-order systems, particularly quaternion-valued neural networks.

My work develops rigorous theoretical frameworks and applies them to complex dynamical behaviors in networked and multi-agent systems, striving to bridge the gap between abstract mathematical theory and applied engineering solutions.

Vision & Impact

Research Statement

My research vision centers on formulating rigorous analytical frameworks to master the behavior of complex, interconnected networks—a pivotal challenge in modern engineering and applied mathematics. By integrating fractional calculus with quaternion algebra, I have established new paradigms for the stability, controllability, and projective synchronization of fractional-order dynamical systems. This foundational work successfully addresses the multi-dimensional complexities inherent in nonidentical and competitive neural networks.

Looking forward, my research strategy is inherently highly interdisciplinary and structurally geared toward securing competitive external funding. I am actively expanding my analytical frameworks into data-driven domains, specifically Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs). The integration of fractional-order modeling with machine learning holds immense potential for creating resilient, adaptable control systems necessary for next-generation intelligent transportation, smart grids, and autonomous multi-agent robotics. My goal is to lead a highly collaborative research laboratory that bridges deep mathematical theory with transformative computational applications.

Thesis Summary

Doctoral Research & Projects

Thesis Title: Synchronization of Fractional-Order Quaternion-Valued Neural Networks

Supervisor: Prof. Muslim Malik | Institution: Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Mandi

During my doctoral tenure, I successfully established novel mathematical approaches for the synchronization of complex neural networks operating within the quaternion field. A significant breakthrough of my thesis involved developing sliding mode control techniques and asymptotic adaptive projective synchronization methods tailored for nonidentical fractional-order systems.

This rigorous exploration resulted in robust theoretical proofs addressing the inconsistencies of fractional orders in hypercomplex spaces, yielding over 7 high-impact peer-reviewed publications in prestigious international journals such as the European Physical Journal Plus and the International Journal of Computer Mathematics.

Pedagogy

Teaching Statement

I view teaching as a dynamic, interactive process of empowering students to connect abstract mathematical concepts with tangible, real-world applications. My pedagogical philosophy is rooted in clarity, active learning, and technological integration. I believe that advanced mathematical literacy is critical for solving modern engineering and computational problems.

In the classroom, I employ a modern, inquiry-based teaching approach that supplements rigorous theoretical instruction with computational tools such as MATLAB and Python. By fostering an inclusive, intellectually stimulating environment, I encourage students to not only master fundamental algorithms but to critically analyze the underlying logic. Whether teaching introductory Linear Algebra or advanced Differential Equations, my objective remains consistent: to equip the next generation of engineers and applied scientists with the analytical rigor required to excel independently in an ever-evolving technological landscape.
